When a virtual representation of an object is manipulated in a similar way to a real-world object
- Drag the scrollbar instead of typing in a command to scroll down

We want to make the interaction feel like we are directly manipulating a "real object" instead of working through something else
- required representation of task objects
- **Benefit**
	- Focus of the task rather than the technology
	- feeling of direct involvement with a world of task objects

**Interaction Model** - a set of principles, rules, and properties that guide the design of an interface
- how to combine interaction techniques in a meaningful and consistent way 
- Can be used to evaluate specific interaction designs

**Instrumental Interaction** - A model of interaction based on how we naturally use tools to manipulate objects in the real world 
- has interaction instruments and domain objects

**Reification** - turning concepts into something concrete

**Meta-instrument** - an instrument that acts on another instrument 

**Object Reification** - Turning attributes of a primary object into other objects of interest

**Describing Instruments** - Three properties
1. Degree of indirection
	- 2D measure of "distance" from instrumentation object (spacial or temporal)
2. Degree of integration
	- Ratio of the degrees of freedom of the instrument over the DOF captured by the input device (ideally 1) and this captures the sustainability of the device
3. Degree of compatibility
	- Similarity of physical actions on instrument and response of the object - more similar is better since its more natural and intuitive
